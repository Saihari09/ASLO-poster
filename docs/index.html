I'm a bioinformatician and microbial ecologist at the Leibniz Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB Berlin), working under Prof. Hans-Peter Grossart on the ODER~SO project. My doctoral work uses spatio-temporal metagenomics — 16 sites × 16 months on the Oder River, sequenced on Illumina and Nanopore — to understand how the 2022 brackish-water disaster and the 2024 Prymnesium parvum bloom reshaped microbial community structure and function.

ASLO–SIL 2026 · Aquatic Confluence · Abstract 11626

Did the Oder microbiome change after a toxic Prymnesium parvum bloom?

Metagenomic characterization of microbial community shifts during Prymnesium blooms in the Oder River

Summer 2022. The Oder River. A toxic Prymnesium parvum bloom and more than 500 km of devastation. Two years on, the river is changed — Prymnesium has become a permanent resident with recurrent blooms. This poster asks whether each bloom reorganises the Oder microbiome along reproducible taxonomic, functional and environmental axes — or passes over a stable community.

About the poster

Successive blooms across 16 months on the Oder

The poster traces the Oder River microbiome through a succession of distinct blooms — a Summer–Autumn 2023 cyanobacterial bloom, a Winter 2023/24 baseline, the June 2024 Prymnesium parvum bloom, and the September 2024 Microcystis bloom — over 16 months of continuous sampling. Using a hybrid Illumina + Nanopore design (HybridSPAdes assemblies, FAIRY → MetaBAT2 binning, CheckM2 / GTDB-Tk / Bakta, and an MMseqs2 linclust gene catalog), it asks whether each bloom period reshapes the microbiome along reproducible taxonomic, functional and environmental axes. Bloom transition timeline & processing pipeline

Four bloom regimes between Jun 2023 and Sep 2024, processed via HybridSPAdes → FAIRY/MetaBAT2 → dRep → MMseqs2 linclust.

Q2

Which microbes track Prymnesium — and what do they encode?

db-RDA on Bray–Curtis phylum distances against environment + bloom + microscopy vectors.

Bloom-associated community gradients

31.3% variance on CAP1 + CAP2; TN / NO3-N / SRP drop during blooms; pH + Chl-a + Salinity + TP form the bloom axis. adj. R² = 35.8%, p = 0.002.

Q3

Do bloom periods reorganise the river microbiome?

Sampling map and time series first, then genus-level winners/losers and the top-50 Prymnesium-correlated MAGs with their encoded capacity.

Sampling map & bloom time series

12 main-channel sites sampled monthly; Prymnesium abundance peaks in June 2024; mOTU genus richness collapses during Prymnesium blooms and recovery is repressed.

Genera & MAGs that track Prymnesium

Oligotrophs (LD12, acIV-A, PnecC) collapse; Roseobacter-clade phycosphere guild rises. B12 salvage + phosphate scavenging dominate the response, with polysaccharide degradation along the Bacteroidota arc and C1 metabolism + B12 biosynthesis along the α-proteobacterial arc.

Q4

How much of the microbiome response is hidden beyond known functions?

A functional view: COG-UMAP, monthly KO-profile trajectory, and the share of bloom-enriched gene clusters with no eggNOG annotation.

COG-functional UMAP

Bloom states occupy distinct functional space; eukaryotic functions dominate the Prymnesium cluster.

Functional trajectory through bloom succession

PC1 captures 31.1% of functional variation; large jumps mark regime shifts; Prymnesium sits in a distinct functional state.

Functional dark matter peaks during Prymnesium

Top-TPM clusters with no eggNOG annotation: 18.0% winter baseline → 28.2% Summer 2023 → 25.1% Microcystis → 46.9% Prymnesium. The bloom response is dominated by uncharacterised genes — +29 pp, 2.6× baseline.

Conclusion

During the June 2024 Prymnesium bloom the Oder microbiome reorganised along environmental and taxonomic axes — freshwater oligotrophs (LD12, acIV-A, PnecC) collapsed while an α-proteobacterial phycosphere guild rose, scavenging B12 and phosphate. ~47% of bloom-enriched gene clusters carry no functional annotation — functional dark matter, 2.6× the winter baseline.
